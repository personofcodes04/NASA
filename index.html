Mission Phases

Six strategic phases to establish humanity's first comprehensive off‑world resource extraction and colonization infrastructure

Phase 1: Technology Demonstration (2027–2030) – 3.5 Years

Station: Orbital Testbed‑1 (OT‑1)

Primary Mission: Validate core technologies in an operational space environment before scaling.

  • Mass Driver Experiments: Three prototype electromagnetic launchers (coilgun, railgun, rotating‑tether hybrid); 100‑gram payloads at 100 m/s; EMI shielding, thermal management, and precision targeting tests.
  • Debris Collection: Two robotic janitors with nets, harpoons, and robotic arms; target 3–5 defunct satellites per year in similar orbits.
  • Material Processing: 2 kW furnace smelting aluminum and steel in microgravity; process debris into ingots with ≥85% purity.
  • Satellite Servicing: 2–3 refuel/repair missions per year; replace batteries, deploy stuck solar panels, refuel hydrazine.
  • Operations Focus (added): Station 1 recycles orbital debris and uses produced materials to provide satellite maintenance. It conducts mass‑driver tests and routinely launches small probes toward the Moon and nearby asteroids.

Phase 2: Pilot Manufacturing Facility (2030–2034) – 4 Years

Station: LEO Manufacturing Platform Alpha (LMP‑Alpha)

Primary Mission: Establish profitable space manufacturing and scale mass driver technology.

  • Manufacturing: 3D‑print components from recycled aluminum; premium optical fiber; protein and semiconductor crystals; produce 2–3 small satellites/year from recycled materials.
  • Mass Driver Scale‑Up: Operational 10 m launcher; 5 kg at 500 m/s; fire rate 1/hr; target small NEO fragments.
  • Asteroid Prospecting: Launch 6 CubeSat prospectors; characterize 15–20 NEAs via spectroscopy and mapping.
  • Debris Processing: Process 80–120 tons/year into feedstock (Al 45%, Ti 8%, Cu 12%, steel 25%).
  • Operational Emphasis (added): Station 2 scales to higher‑mass launches and increases production capacity. It represents a key step toward building future stations in space, with many parts manufactured from recycled orbital debris.

Phase 3: Commercial Asteroid Mining (2034–2040) – 6 Years

Station: Artemis Resource Extraction Hub

Primary Mission: Begin economically viable asteroid mining and stockpile resources for deep space missions.

  • Robotic Mining Fleet: 8 autonomous spacecraft; 3‑year life; target mostly C‑type and some M‑type; surface excavation; return via mass driver capsules.
  • Advanced Mass Driver: 50 m track; 150 kg at 3 km/s; ±0.1° targeting; dual‑use for returns and cislunar logistics.
  • Resource Processing: Water 180–240 t/yr; PGM 15–25 kg/yr; rare earths 200–400 kg/yr; bulk metals 800 t/yr.
  • Colonization Support: Manufacture 12 lunar habitat modules; stockpile 50 t water in orbital depot; 95% life‑support recycling tests.
  • Program Focus (added): With maturing mass‑driver experiments, truly heavy probes are launched. Nearby asteroids are gently redirected and processed to supply raw materials that boost Station 2’s manufacturing output.
  • Asteroid Capture Strategy (added): Robot probes impact and secure a safe fragment, then use ion thrusters to bring it to LEO. As the fragment approaches the collection station, a secondary drone intercepts to slow it for a controlled, safe capture by the station.

Phase 4: Deep Space Logistics Network (2040–2047) – 7 Years

Station: Gateway Logistics Network (GLN‑Prime)

Primary Mission: Become the central hub for cislunar and Mars‑bound logistics, minimizing Earth launches.

  • Interplanetary Supply Chain: Propellant depot 800–1200 t/yr; contracts with Artemis and private programs; mass driver shipments to Moon and Mars; lunar L1 waystation.
  • Heavy Manufacturing: 20 m tanks, 50 m trusses, solar arrays, ion and nuclear thermal engines; 4–6 transfer vehicles/year.
  • Expanded Mining: 24 probes; larger M‑type targets; experimental asteroid redirection and shepherding.
  • Mass Driver Array: Four tracks; 800 kg at 5 km/s; weekly lunar, monthly Mars launches; lunar catcher system.
  • Strategic Direction (added): Target more distant, higher‑grade asteroids and minimize Earth‑based space manufacturing.

Phase 5: Autonomous Shipyard & Industrial Complex (2047–2056) – 9 Years

Station: Prometheus Orbital Manufacturing Collective (POMC)

Primary Mission: Construct interplanetary spacecraft entirely from space‑sourced materials.

  • Spacecraft Construction: 3–5 vessels/year, 1000–3000 t; nuclear thermal propulsion; rotating habs; closed‑loop life support.
  • Mega‑Structures: Begin 5 km Stanford torus; solar power satellite components (200 MW pilot).
  • Advanced Materials: Microgravity metallurgy, ultra‑pure silicon, graphene, biomaterials.
  • Autonomous Mining: 50+ robots; AI prospecting; mothership processors; pilot water mining at Psyche.
  • Colonization Step (added): Asteroid mining reaches sufficient scale and colonization begins. Mass drivers routinely launch to the Moon and Mars so crews can depart Earth with minimal mass.

Phase 6: Solar System Infrastructure Federation (2056–2070) – 14 Years

Station: Sol Industrial Federation Network (SIFN)

Primary Mission: Establish self‑sustaining industrial ecosystems across the Solar System; Earth transitions to coordination role.

  • Multi‑Body Network: LEO hub admin & R&D; lunar industry (He‑3, Ti, REE); Mars orbit & surface complexes; belt outposts; Venus aerostat lab; Mercury solar farm.
  • Mass Driver Network: 18 drivers creating an interplanetary highway; LEO→Luna cargo in 2 days; LEO→Mars in 3 months; power beaming experiments.
  • Mega‑Structures: Stanford torus complete (10k residents); start O'Neill Cylinder; 12 SBSP satellites (12 GW).
  • Economic Transformation: 90% of spacecraft never touch Earth; space GDP $400B/yr; new industries flourish.
  • Ops Evolution (added): A continuously operating, more advanced 6th‑generation station comes online and gradually retires the first two stations while manufacturing next‑generation facilities.
